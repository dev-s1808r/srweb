But why?

I don't remember exactly when the thought of building a website popped up in my mind. It wasn't as if I didn't have a digital presence. In fact, I have been present on digital media for a long time. I remember opening my account on Orkut way back in 2005 or 2006 (I was in school then, and I 'mistakenly' entered my age as above 18!). Then I wrote my first blog on Blogger in 2007. I was still in school then. I uploaded my first YouTube video, probably in 2010 or may be 2011. I was playing a Hindi song on my guitar in the video. Then, of course, came Facebook. I had a couple of accounts on Facebook. I was, and I still am, on LinkedIn and Twitter as well. These days, I primarily use Instagram.

Most of my digital presence on these sites has been limited to sharing photos, videos, or write-ups. It was by accident that I realized that I like clicking photos. My dad eventually bought me a camera, and my love for photography flourished, and my misadventures in video making also started. Writing, on the other hand, was actually a natural extension of my thought process. I used to pen my thoughts when I felt the clutter was too much to process. I didn't keep count of the photos I clicked because there were too many. Videos, on the other hand, were too few to count. But I did count my write-ups once, and the number was around 150 in total.

So, considering all this, I can fairly conclude that a "nee-to-express" was never really the reason for me to build my website because I have been expressing anyway, and, in fact, in many ways. It wasn't a desire to create some digital monument either. That said, I vaguely remember thinking, "Hey! I should have one place where all the content that I have created is available in one repository, and I should be able to decide the flow, the narrative, the aesthetics, and whatnot."

My initial reaction to this, which I remember only vaguely, was that of skepticism—mostly because it made me feel as if I were some control freak who was insecure about losing control over his narrative (by the way, I go by pronouns he/him)—and partly because the effort of building such a thing from scratch was overwhelming, and I just didn't know where to start or how to design it and the rest. So, I kept procrastinating on the thought of building my website until the switch somehow flipped, and I was suddenly filled with the motivation of "building my own digital abode," "a corner in the vast universe of zeros and ones," "a place where everyone could find me," "a testament to the brand Sourabh Raghavendra." I don't know when it happened; I don't remember what caused it (although, knowing myself, I could say I may have seen some stunning portfolio website that made me want to build something like that). I don't know.

But then, that journey, too, wasn't easy either. I went through several iterations of design, of content, of structure. I wrote a lot, and then erased it all! I started with the cursor blinking on a blank page until the screen turned off to save battery, and then, unwillingly, I began with my frustrated face too! Until very recently, I used to brush all this frustration under the rug of "writer's block," and it used to make me feel a little better in the (literal) face of frustration, but then at some point, I realized that if I had to move past this phase, I had to gather some courage to accept the fact that I was over-obsessed with perfection to the degree of intellectual paralysis. I was somehow not okay with something that was simple. I used to "feel" that complexity was the ultimate sophistication. Well, it may or may not be. I don't know. One thing, however, that I know is that it doesn't help you build your website.

With all this in mind, one fine late night (February 24, 2025), I sat down with a question and a decision.

Question: Why do I want to build a website?

Decision: The answer to this question will be the first page of my website.

So, here it is: my website: https://sourabhraghavendra.in

I will be posting more and more content for sure.

If I forget, feel free to ping me at connect@sourabhraghavendra.in to remind me that it has been days since I last updated my website.
